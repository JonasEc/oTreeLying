Overview of the study:

For this study you will throw a set of {{Constants.numberOfDice}} dice {{Constants.num_rounds}} times. You will need to record the results of your dice throws and add them up.

Hence, to complete this study, you will need to have access to some paper and a pen or pencil to write down your results. You may also wish to use a calculator to add up your die throws - but we will provide one for you to use, too. If you want to throw real dice, you will need at one die which you can throw {{Constants.numberOfDice}} times, recording the results for each separate throw. Alternatively, you can also use this website.

If you like, you can experiment with the website to see that it produces a fair dice throw now.

Please gather all your supplies now.

How to add up the dice throws: :
Dice are added up just the way you would think: count the eyes on each die and simply sum up the results.

How is your payment determined?
In addition to your HIT reward of {{Constants.HIT}}, we will pay you for one of your {{Constants.num_rounds}} dice throws. The computer will randomly select one of the rounds. The selection will not depend on your answer to any question in this experiment.

Generally, the larger the sum of your die throws the luckier you got - we will pay you more! At most you may earn an addtional {{extra}}! The exact payment rule will differ between rounds. We will describe the rule before you report your results for each throw.

Beliefs about others:
We are also interested to find out what you believe other players will have thrown. For this, we would like you to report what you think is the median report of all other workers completing this task who saw the same payment rule as you did.
(Quick reminder: the median is the middle number of a list of numbers, after you sorted numbers from large to small or from small to large. For example, for the 9 numbers 17,25,27,29,30,34,36,39,51, the median is 30 - four numbers are smaller than 30, and four are bigger than 30.)
We will compare all guesses to the actual median after the study has been completed and will pay you an additional bonus of {{Constants.guessBonus}} if you were within plus/minus one of the true median in the round that was selected for payment!

Survey:
At the end of the experiment, we will ask you a couple of quick questions about yourself. We appreciate your honest and thoughtful responses to these questions for our research!
